What it does & how it works

Foam to Farm

We convert clean foam packaging into modular plant‑growth structures. Laminated foam panels are cut into trays, raft boards, channel spacers, and vertical frames. Panels are lined with monomaterial food‑wrap films from packaging to create a safe barrier between nutrient solution and foam. Printed joints and clamps snap panels onto the repurposed transport cube, which becomes the FabRack for hydroponics. The same rack holds pumps, tubing, and sensors, with the foam also serving as vibration and thermal insulation.

Plastic to Filament

All suitable plastic films and wraps are pressed, shredded, filtered, and extruded into filament. We print connectors, net‑pot collars, manifold brackets, hose clips, elbows, organizers, and storage boxes that support the farm and daily habitat needs.

Benefits

  • Food infrastructure without new shipped hardware
  • Lower waste volume and stowage
  • Parts on demand with on‑site fabrication
  • Reuses process heat to pre‑warm nutrient solution / seed trays
  • Low crew time and minimal water use
  • Closed particle handling; no burning
  • Greens interior spaces, improving well‑being
  • Stabilizes spares via printable components

Intended impact

A practical circular pathway that turns two abundant waste streams into life‑support assets at Jezero. It reduces logistics mass and volume, stabilizes spares through on‑site fabrication, and improves crew well‑being by greening interior spaces.

Tools, coding languages, hardware & software

  • CAD: Fusion 360 or Onshape; parametric parts in OpenSCAD; slicing with Cura or PrusaSlicer.
  • Analysis: Quick material balance & sizing in Python notebooks.
  • Hardware: Compact shredder; small filament extruder with diameter sensing; FFF 3D printer; heated press/plates for lamination; hand tools; simple bins or CTBs for segregation and tracking.
  • Controls (optional): Microcontroller interlocks for temperature & safety.

What makes it creative

Foam packaging becomes the backbone of a hydroponic rig, lined with reclaimed films for safety and durability. The transport cube is not waste but a zero‑extra‑mass workbench. One waste stream feeds another: plastic films become filament that prints the very joints that hold the farm together.

Factors considered

  • Food safety via film liners & easy‑to‑clean surfaces
  • Segregation of bio‑contact textiles
  • Dust & particle control during shredding
  • Low water use via recirculation
  • Reuse of process heat
  • Maintenance & cleaning of tools
  • Simple QC for filament diameter
  • Traceability of recycled lots

Team ReLife: Summary

ReLife turns post‑landing materials into life‑support hardware. We focus on two streams at Jezero: foam packaging and plastic films. Foam becomes modular plant‑growth structures: laminated trays, channel spacers, and vertical frames lined with reclaimed food films, mounted on the repurposed transport cube as a FabRack for hydroponics. Plastic films are pressed, shredded, and extruded into filament to 3D print the joints, clips, and organizers that hold the farm and daily tools together.

The workflow is straightforward: sort, light sterilize, thermally compact or laminate, extrude, print, and assemble. It runs with little crew time, uses very little water, contains particles, and avoids burning. The payoff is smaller waste volume, fewer shipped spares, and a steadier supply of useful parts, while greenery improves crew well‑being. ReLife is a clear, scalable path to circular operations on Mars.

Resources & Safety Compliance

  • Electricity: Assumed continuous per brief; no separate generation/storage included.
  • Water minimization: Squeegee + mist rinse; closed‑loop wash with staged filters; warm‑air drying via reclaimed waste heat.
  • Reuse of outputs: Extruder/press waste heat ducted to drying rack / rinse pre‑heater; warm rinse used to pre‑heat lamination plates via simple heat‑exchanger coil.
  • Crew time: Semi‑automatic pouch slitter‑flattener; quick‑change cutting dies; standardized jigs; QR checklists; batch processing.
  • Regolith (MGS‑1): Used as sealed ballast in printed bases; optional pigment in fully encapsulated parts (no loose dust).
  • No burning/incineration; PFAS & toxics: Exclude fluorinated grease‑resistant wrappers; process only known mono‑PE/PP/PET streams for extrusion. Multi‑layer foil pouches are used as sheets only.
  • Microplastics: Edges thermal‑sealed; closed‑loop filtration captures fines; filters disposed as sealed solid waste. No wastewater discharge.
  • Food safety: Celebration items avoid direct food contact unless lined with known food‑grade mono‑film; all food‑contact doubtful items tagged non‑food.
